Juan José García Cárdenas

I am a PhD student in the Autonomous Systems and Robotics lab, Computer Sciences and Systems Engineering Department (U2IS) at ENSTA Paris, Institute Polytechnique de Paris (IP Paris). My PhD supervisor is Prof. Adriana Tapus. My research focuses on measuring how the collaborative tasks between humans and robots affect the cognitive load and trust of humans in robots. Also, the main idea of the project is to develop a machine-learning framework that allows robots to improve the cognitive load and trust of the operator. I'm part of the European RAICAM Project

Institute Polytechnic de Paris - ENSTA

PhD in Computer Science and Robotics | December 2023 - Ongoing (Expected: December 2026)

Dissertation: "Performance, Trust, Workload, and Social Processes in a Human-Robot Collaborative Task"

Keywords: Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), trust modeling, cognitive load/workload, teleoperation, shared autonomy, Bayesian/probabilistic modeling, user studies.

Thesis summary: Investigates how performance, trust, and workload evolve during human-robot collaboration and teleoperation, and how these signals can be modeled (e.g., probabilistically) to adapt autonomy and information presentation for safer, more effective interaction.

ENRICH 2025 - European Robotics Hackathon
